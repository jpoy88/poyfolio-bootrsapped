New Website in Progress


“We have to start somewhere. It doesn't matter if it wasn't as good as we imagined. Just keep playing.”

06.14.14


Iam pretty sure that every beginner with coding wanted to start a portfolio website in conjuction with learning HTML and CSS. I am not going to lie, I cried a bit learning CSS. We have this very resistive relationship. Yet, I am still trying to learn and persevere while CSS is trying to avoid compromise. As a design enthusiast, this should have my strongest interest in coding languages. Me and CSS will be bestfriends someday, more like partner in crime.

With the help of Team Treehouse and Codecademy plus persistence, the emotional barrier of learning will fade at some point. Team Treehouse is excellent at showing detailed knowledge and how can you incorporate codes to different activities. I am a tactile or hands-on learner, Codecademy is perfect for doing drills and gaining code experience. They have projects to play with and I can attest that I learn a lot. Promise, they are not boring projects.


  • Price:
  • Team Treehouse: $25/mo standard. $9/mo for student and veterans.
  • Codecademy: FREE.

To clarify, I am not getting paid to talk about these two websites. It would be nice to share my first step so you have an idea where to start.

However, it is unfortunate to say that I'm going to slow down learning HTML & CSS because I will be switching full focus on Ruby. I recently applied for App Academy and going to take my chances joining the coding bootcamp.
